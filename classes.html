Classes & schedule

Weekly classes for children & adults, in two cities.

Term runs from September through June, mirroring the Belgian school calendar. A full schedule is below; please write to confirm a place before your first class.

Every student begins with a fifteen-minute conversation — about why you are here, what you have studied, and what you hope for. From there we agree on the right group.

I.

Indian Folk Dances — Maharashtra · Rajasthan · all levels

A living survey of two regional traditions. Maharashtrian forms — lavani, koli, dindi — alongside the Rajasthani ghoomar and kalbelia. Beginners start with stance, rhythm and simple steps; intermediate and advanced groups build toward full sequences with prop and costume conventions. Bollywood is taught as a separate course and is not part of this curriculum.

Beginner · Intermediate · Advanced 75 minutes
II.

Bharatanatyam — भरतनाट्यम् · beginner to intermediate

Adavus, the ten basic stances, and a slow accumulation of jathis. Theory of rhythm (tala) is taught alongside the movement from the outset.

Beginner · Intermediate 75 minutes
III.

Children (7–12) — foundations group

A playful, unhurried introduction. Posture, rhythm, short stories drawn from epics. Small group so that each child can be seen and corrected kindly.

Mixed levels 60 minutes
